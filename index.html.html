03 — The Filter
Five questions i asked while prospecting.
1
Is the buyer a LinkedIn-native professional?
If their buyers aren't scrolling LinkedIn, Creatorbuzz's creator network can't reach them. CTOs and engineers out. VP Sales, VP Marketing, Head of Demand Gen, in.
2
Is the category crowded enough that differentiation is urgent?
Sales tech, HR tech, marketing tech: noisy spaces where a human face on your brand is a real edge.
3
Are they already feeling the pain of paid ads?
Heavy event presence, organic content that's quiet despite the spend: classic signals of diminishing returns.
4
20–200 employees, Series A or B?
Small enough to move fast. Large enough to have real budget. This is the decision-maker window.
5
Is there a visible content gap?
Strong product, quiet organic presence; means they're ready to invest, not doing it out of habit.
04 — The Lead
After disregarding Warmly, i settled on this company:
RB2B
Intentionally Bootstrapped
Buyer profile: VP Sales & VP Marketing at SMB B2B: exactly who lives on LinkedIn and trusts creator content. The ideal buyer we can reach at RB2B is someone like founder Adam Robinson. He views marketing as entertainment first. They don't want a traditional agency; they want a "hit-maker." From my research, their profile is high-risk, high-reward, and they are comfortable with polarizing content if it drives high-volume signups for their free tier.
The pain is public: RB2B's biggest pain is it's founder-dependency. While Adam's personal brand is a powerhouse, if he stops posting, the brand's primary growth engine stalls. They need to decentralize their influence. Creatorbuzz can solve this by recruiting external creators to help grow the brand beyond its founder.
The irony of the pitch: RB2B sells software that tells you exactly who is visiting your website, yet they often struggle to consistently reach new audiences on social platforms without manual effort. We can pitch them the ability to "de-anonymize" the brand to the masses through influencers, essentially doing for RB2B's brand what RB2B's software does for its customers' websites.
Content-aware: RB2B is already "content-aware"; they know that "boring" B2B marketing is a death sentence. However, they need an agency that understands the specific nuances of SaaS video loops and LinkedIn Algorithm hacks. Creatorbuzz fits because beyond making videos; we create content designed to be amplified as Thought Leader ads, a format RB2B already aggressively uses.
Crowded category: sales tech is noisy. Since Creatorbuzz has already proven they can win this specific battle for Warmly, we have the tested approach choice to help RB2B.
